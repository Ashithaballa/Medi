Medical Treatment and Procedures

We offer a variety of advanced treatments and procedures tailored to each patientâ€™s needs:

  • Cardiac Medications: These medications are crucial for treating various heart conditions, helping to manage symptoms, improve cardiac function, and prevent complications. They are commonly used for controlling high blood pressure, alleviating chest pain, and correcting abnormal heart rhythms.
  • Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG): This surgical procedure creates new pathways for blood to flow around blocked or narrowed arteries, improving blood supply to the heart.
  • Percutaneous Transluminal Coronary Angioplasty (PTCA): A minimally invasive procedure that uses a balloon to widen blocked blood vessels, often followed by the placement of a stent to keep the arteries open.
  • Pacemaker Implantation: Insertion of a device that helps regulate abnormal heart rhythms by sending electrical impulses to the heart.
  • Ventricular Assist Device (VAD) Implantation: A mechanical pump that supports heart function and blood flow in patients with severe heart failure.
  • Heart Transplantation: A surgical procedure where a failing heart is replaced with a healthy heart from a donor.
  • Heart Valve Repair or Replacement: Surgical interventions to repair or replace damaged heart valves, including procedures like mitral valve replacement, trans catheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR), surgical aortic valve replacement (SAVR), and pulmonary valve surgery.
  • Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillator (ICD): A device used in cardiac resynchronization therapy to correct irregular heart rhythms and improve the synchronization of the heart chambers.
  • Cardiac Ablation: A procedure that destroys abnormal heart tissue responsible for irregular heartbeats, using techniques such as radiofrequency energy or cryotherapy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the common symptoms of heart disease?

Common symptoms include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, palpitations (irregular heartbeats), fatigue, and dizziness.

What are risk factors for heart disease?

Risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, lack of physical activity, and family history of heart disease.

When should I see a cardiologist?

You should consider seeing a cardiologist if you experience symptoms like chest pain, shortness of breath, palpitations, or if you have risk factors for heart disease.

What diagnostic tests are used in cardiology?

Diagnostic tests may include electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG), echocardiogram, stress test, cardiac catheterization, CT scan, and MRI of the heart.

What treatments are available for heart conditions?

Treatments may include medications (e.g., for blood pressure or cholesterol), lifestyle changes (diet, exercise), cardiac procedures (angioplasty, stenting), and heart surgery (bypass surgery, valve replacement).

How can I prevent heart disease?

Prevention strategies include maintaining a healthy diet, regular exercise, managing stress, quitting smoking, controlling blood pressure and cholesterol levels, and regular check-ups with your doctor.

What should I expect during a cardiology consultation?

During a consultation, the cardiologist will review your medical history, perform a physical examination, discuss your symptoms and concerns, and may recommend further tests or treatments based on their evaluation.
